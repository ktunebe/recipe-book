Ingredients


  • 1 lb yukon gold potatoes
  • 2 large carrots
  • 2 stalks celery
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 1 Tbsp flour
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 Tbsp sour cream
  • 1.5 oz tomato paste
  • 10 oz ground beef
  • 2 beef bullion cubes, dissolved in 3/4 cup hot water
  • 1/2 cup grated or shredded white cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions


  1. Heat broiler to high.
  2. Dice potatoes into 1/2-inch pieces. Trim, peel and halve carrots lenthwise, then slice into 1/4 inch slices. Finely dice celery. Peel and finely chop onion.
  3. Place potatoes in a medium pot with enough water to cover by 2 inches. Bring to a boil and cook until tender, 15-20 minutes. Drain and return potatoes to pot.
  4. Mash potatoes with sour cream, butter and 1/2 tsp thyme until smooth, adding splashes of water as needed. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. While potatoes cook, heat a drizzle of oil in a medium over-proof pan (large if making double serving) over medium-high heat. Add carrot, season with salt and pepper and cook 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add celery, onion, and a large drizzle of oil to pan with carrots, season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, until veggies are tender, 5-7 minutes.
  7. Stir in garlic powder and 1/2 tsp thyme and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  8. Add beef to pan with veggies and season with salt and pepper. Cook, breaking up meat into pieces, until browned and cooked through, about 4-6 minutes.
  9. Add tomato paste and flour and cook, stirring, until thoroughly combined, 1 minute.
  10. Gradually pour in bullion-water mixture and bring to a boil. Cook until mixture is very thick, 1-2 minutes. Turn off heat.
  11. Top beef filling with an even layer of mashed potatoes, leaving a gap around the edge of the pan. Sprinkle evenly with cheddar.
  12. Broil until browned, 3-4 minutes. Serve directly from pan.
